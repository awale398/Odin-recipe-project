Biryani is a rich, aromatic rice dish made by layering spiced meat (like chicken, beef, goat, or lamb) with fragrant basmati rice, herbs, and sometimes saffron. Originating from South Asia, it‚Äôs popular in Kenya, especially along the coast, and is often served during celebrations, weddings, and special family meals. Biryani is loved for its deep flavor, vibrant color, and satisfying blend of spices and textures.

Step-by-step cooking instructions

üçó Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

  1. In a large bowl, mix chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, turmeric, chili powder, garam masala, and salt.
  2. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (overnight for best flavor).

üçö Step 2: Prepare the Rice

  1. Rinse basmati rice until water runs clear; soak for 20‚Äì30 minutes.
  2. In a pot, bring water to boil with salt, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and bay leaf.
  3. Add soaked rice and boil until it's about 70% cooked (firm but not soft).
  4. Drain and set aside.

üçõ Step 3: Make the Biryani Base

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a large pot or sufuria.
  2. Fry onions until golden brown; set half aside for garnishing.
  3. Add cumin seeds and cook for 30 seconds.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and saucy.
  5. Add marinated chicken and cook until the chicken is tender and the sauce thickens. (Add water if needed).

ü´ï Step 4: Layering

  1. In the same pot (or another), layer the cooked chicken mixture at the bottom.
  2. Add a layer of rice on top.
  3. Sprinkle with fried onions, chopped coriander, mint leaves, and saffron milk (optional).
  4. Repeat layers if needed.

üî• Step 5: Final Steam (Dum)

  1. Cover the pot tightly with a lid or foil.
  2. Cook on low heat for 15‚Äì20 minutes (you can place a heavy pan below the pot to avoid burning).
  3. Let it rest 5 minutes before opening.

‚úÖ Step 6: Serve

  1. Gently fluff the biryani with a fork.
  2. Serve hot with raita, kachumbari, or salad.

‚úÖ Biryani Cooking Tips:

  1. Marinate Longer for Flavor:
    • Let the meat marinate for at least 1 hour‚Äîovernight gives the best flavor and tenderness.
  2. Use Long-Grain Basmati Rice:
    • Soak it for 20‚Äì30 minutes before cooking to prevent it from breaking and to achieve fluffy grains.
  3. Partially Cook Rice (70%):
    • Don‚Äôt fully cook the rice before layering. This ensures it finishes cooking with the steam during dum (final simmering).
  4. Use Whole Spices for Aroma:
    • Add cloves, cardamom, bay leaf, and cinnamon to both the rice and meat for rich fragrance.
  5. Fry Onions Until Golden-Brown:
    • Crispy fried onions add flavor, sweetness, and texture‚Äîreserve some for the final garnish.
  6. Seal the Pot Well (Dum):
    • Cover tightly with foil or a heavy lid to trap steam for even cooking and rich flavor fusion.
  7. Saffron or Food Color (Optional):
    • Soak a few strands of saffron in warm milk and drizzle over the rice for color and aroma, or use a pinch of food color.
  8. Let It Rest:
    • After cooking, let biryani sit covered for 5‚Äì10 minutes before serving to allow flavors to settle.

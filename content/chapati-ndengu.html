Ingredients list

For Chapati

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Warm water (as needed)
  • Extra flour and oil for rolling and cooking

For Ndengu

  • 1 cup green grams (mung beans)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 clove garlic (optional), minced
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 clove garlic (optional), minced

Step-by-step cooking instructions

How to cook Chapati

  1. Mix Dough
    • In a bowl, combine flour, salt, sugar (optional), and mix well.
    • Add water gradually while kneading until a soft, non-sticky dough forms.
    • Add a bit of oil and knead again for 2â€“3 minutes.
  2. Let the Dough Rest
    • Cover the dough with a cloth and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Roll and Shape
    • Divide the dough into equal balls.
    • Roll out each ball into a flat circle on a floured surface.
  4. Cook Chapatis
    • Heat a pan (non-stick or skillet) on medium heat.
    • Place one rolled chapati on the pan and cook for about 30 seconds.
    • Flip, apply a little oil, and cook both sides until golden brown with light brown spots.
    • Repeat for all dough balls.

  4. Serve Hot
    • Serve hot ndengu with soft, warm chapatis.

Cooking Tips

  • Soak ndengu for a few hours or overnight to cook faster.
  • Simmer ndengu until soft before adding to the sautÃ©ed onions and tomatoes.
  • For softer chapatis, let the dough rest for at least 30 minutes.
  • Use enough oil while cooking chapatis to get flaky, golden layers.
  • Pair with kachumbari (tomato-onion salad) for added flavor and freshness.
